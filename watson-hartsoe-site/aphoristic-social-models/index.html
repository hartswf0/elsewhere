Growing Entanglements

Captain Cook, cultural rationality, and seven small worlds.

Fall 2024

The simulations isolate mechanisms. They do not reconstruct Hawaiian experience or settle the Sahlins–Obeyesekere debate.

01

The event comes before the model.

On 17 January 1779, the Resolution and Discovery entered Kealakekua Bay during Makahiki, a season associated with Lono, peace, tribute, and ritual movement. Cook’s arrival and circuit around the islands could be read inside that order.

The ships left after the festival. A storm damaged the Resolution’s mast, and they returned on 12 February. Relations had changed. A cutter was taken. On 14 February, Cook tried to bring Kalaniʻōpuʻu aboard as leverage for its return. A crowd gathered at the water. Cook was killed.

ARRIVALWelcome, gifts, ritual timing, and thousands gathered around the bay.
RETURNStorm damage, a changed season, fraying relations, and a missing cutter.
CONFRONTATIONAn attempted hostage-taking, a crowd at the shore, and Cook’s death.
02

One death enters two causal worlds.

Sahlins places Cook inside Hawaiian cosmology. The timing of Makahiki, the circuit around the islands, and the ritual sequence shape what action could mean.

Obeyesekere objects that the god-Cook account risks repeating a European myth about Indigenous irrationality. Hawaiians appear instead as practical actors responding to theft, force, rank, and immediate danger.

The archive can remain the same while the agents, motives, and causes change.

03

A sentence becomes a distribution.

PLoT translates language into a probabilistic world. In the familiar tug-of-war example, the system never observes strength. It observes wins and losses, then infers the hidden qualities that could have produced them.

The move becomes difficult at Kealakekua Bay. “Strength” is already a recognized variable in tug-of-war. “Lono,” “interest,” “taboo,” and “rationality” are contested descriptions of the world being modeled.

The program does not receive history. It receives a translation.

04

PIE keeps three disagreements in view.

The Practical–Individual–Entropic framework does not decide which reading is true. It gives the disagreement handles.

Practical pressure
Symbolic obligation
Individual choice
Collective schema
Breakdown
Reorganization

This setting holds practical and symbolic, individual and collective, breakdown and reorganization in tension.

A boat can be stolen as material leverage and still matter inside a sacred order. A chief can calculate and act through collective categories. Conflict can destroy one arrangement while producing another.

05

Seven mechanisms, shown plainly.

None of these models is Captain Cook’s death. Each isolates a rule that can help or mislead when it is carried into a historical encounter.

Academic resource 01Epstein · 2002 · Civil violence

The crowd is quiet until risk changes.

Citizens carry hardship and risk aversion. Low legitimacy raises grievance. Nearby police raise expected arrest risk. Activation occurs when grievance wins.

Concrete rule: a person with high hardship can remain quiet for many steps, then activate when nearby rebels increase and the local police ratio falls.

Academic resource 02Axelrod · 1997 · Cultural dissemination

Similarity invites copying.

Agents carry several cultural traits. The more traits two neighbors share, the more likely they are to interact. When they do, one difference may disappear.

Concrete rule: two neighbors sharing four of five traits interact often; the fifth trait may be copied, making the pair identical.

Academic resource 03Schelling · 1971 · Segregation

Small moves divide the whole city.

Households inspect nearby households. Those below a similarity threshold move to an empty cell. Mild preferences accumulate into strong separation.

Concrete rule: a household moves when fewer than one-third of its occupied neighbors are similar, though it never asks for a segregated city.

Academic resource 04Watts & Strogatz · 1998 · Small worlds

A few long ties collapse distance.

Most links stay local. A small number are rewired across the network. Clustering remains, but average paths become short.

Concrete rule: one shortcut can join two dense neighborhoods that previously required many intermediate contacts.

Academic resource 05Cioffi-Revilla & Rouleau · 2009 · RebeLand

Resistance persists where power reaches badly.

Terrain, institutions, resources, and recruitment interact across space. Rough ground and weak governance can shelter insurgent growth.

Concrete rule: high terrain difficulty and low institutional strength let a small red cluster survive long enough to recruit its neighbors.

Academic resource 06Doran & Lees · 2005 · IRUBA

Harm can manufacture allegiance.

Fear, anger, resources, terrain, and military action shift civilian support. Collateral damage moves people toward insurgents; care and equitable provision can move them back.

Concrete rule: repeated harm in one neighborhood changes local allegiance even when the force producing the harm claims the opposite aim.

Academic resource 07Green & Costion · 2018 · Cross-cultural interaction

A border can become a third culture.

Trade, conflict, assimilation, and geography change the boundary between groups. Contact may preserve separation, move the border, or create hybrid practice.

Concrete rule: repeated exchange at a frontier produces mixed cells rather than forcing every cell to become one side or the other.

06

A rule should have a traceable past.

Narrative evidence does not become an agent rule by itself. Someone names an actor, selects a motive, fixes a threshold, and decides what counts as an outcome.

Consider one line: sacred wood was taken. A material reading may encode uncompensated seizure. A symbolic reading may encode a breach of ritual space. A hybrid model may allow both to alter trust, obligation, and collective response.

Ologs can make the mappings explicit. They cannot make the mapping innocent.

07

The model changes what it can see.

Barad calls the boundary that produces a phenomenon an agential cut. In a simulation, the cut appears wherever the modeler decides that a person is an agent, a rite is a belief, a conflict is an output, or a relationship can be held constant.

A clean result may describe the rule more clearly than it describes the world.

08

Four interfaces test where theory resists form.

The project also built four apparatuses around the Cook debate. Each makes one relation visible and damages another.

Spectral NegotiationShift the weight of practical and symbolic readings. Information Architecture NavigatorTurn Sahlins’ narrative into entities, actions, and relations. Flux FieldWatch theory harden into nodes, edges, and force. Narrative LogLet simple rules write a history, then compare it with the archive.

The slider separates rational from symbolic too cleanly. The hierarchy makes culture rigid. The force graph makes disagreement look like geometry. The log makes the rules legible by thinning the history.

Cook’s death can be grown from practical agents. It can also be grown from ritual obligations. A successful run does not choose between them.

History does not enter code whole. It arrives through a hand.
